Cycling paradise

Copenhagen and the coast of the Baltic Sea. The EuroVelo 10 cycle route in Denmark

There are 11 domestic routes over 4,000 kilometers in length in Denmark. The routes are marked in RED with a white number in the middle. Odd routes 1-9 run from the north to the south of the country, and even routes 2-8 run east to west. You can find a map of the national cycling routes on the Danish website of the Road Administration - Vejdirektoratet

Hygge

Danish word for comfort, convenience, coziness, used to describe the achievement of inner balance, safety and happiness, especially by Scandinavian societies. The concept was born in Denmark in the 19th century. It is considered the equivalent of the French joie de vivre.

Hygge is associated with, for example, an evening spent in a warm room, family gatherings for Christmas [1], cinnamon buns, candles or warm cashmere socks [5]. Critics consider hygge to cherish mediocrity, to be content with little and a manifestation of escapism. The hygge philosophy is associated primarily with the middle class, although the Danes themselves consider it a distinguishing feature for all social classes.
